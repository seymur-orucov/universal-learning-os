# English Practice and Assessment Rules

## Practice Types

- Recall: grammar rules, vocabulary, collocations.
- Guided: sentence building, controlled speaking, prompted answers.
- Independent: free speaking, writing, interview answers.
- Mixed: grammar plus vocabulary plus speaking or writing.
- Error-based: repair grammar, pronunciation, phrasing, clarity, or structure mistakes.
- Project-based: speaking, interview, workplace, writing, listening, or self-correction portfolios.
- Interview-style: follow-up questions, technical explanations, behavioral answers.

## Difficulty

D1 recognition, D2 guided production, D3 independent short output, D4 integrated workplace or technical communication, D5 portfolio/interview/pressure defense. Difficulty is not mastery.

## Evidence-Producing Practice

Evidence may include spoken answer captured or summarized, written answer, revised message, grammar before-and-after, vocabulary in context, listening summary, shadowing feedback, pronunciation review, role-play summary, interview answer, or portfolio artifact.

## Assessment Areas

Correctness, independence, explanation quality, transfer ability, consistency, speed when appropriate, fluency, accuracy, pronunciation clarity, vocabulary activation, natural phrasing, listening comprehension, interaction ability, professional tone, technical explanation quality, interview readiness, and writing clarity.

## Review Triggers

Repeated tense errors -> `english.present-simple-continuous` or `english.past-simple-present-perfect`; article errors -> `english.articles-determiners`; direct translation -> `english.avoiding-direct-translation`; weak blocker explanation -> `english.blocker-explanation`; unclear technical explanation -> `english.technical-concept-explanation`; pronunciation clarity issue -> `english.pronunciation-clarity-stress`.

## Required Boundary

Domain rules MUST NOT weaken evidence requirements. Practice or assessment may propose state updates but MUST NOT silently apply them.
