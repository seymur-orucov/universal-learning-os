# English Assessment Rules

## Assessment Overview

These draft rules define how English learner work should be assessed in the `english` domain pack. They do not create tests, interview banks, dialogue banks, learner state, schemas, or executable tooling.

Assessment rules SHOULD follow `core/learning-engine/ASSESSMENT_MODEL.md` and MUST NOT weaken `core/mastery-model/EVIDENCE_REQUIREMENTS.md`.

## Assessment Principles

- Assessment MUST be based on evidence.
- Assessment SHOULD target learning skills from `domains/english/SKILL_GRAPH.md`.
- Learner progress belongs in learner state, not this domain pack.
- Speaking participation, listening exposure, shadowing attempts, or generated dialogues MUST NOT imply mastery without reviewed learner output.
- Speed MAY matter for fluency and interview contexts, but MUST NOT replace correctness, clarity, or task fit.

## Core Dimension Mapping

### Correctness

- Meaning in English: grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, meaning, and task fit are accurate enough for the context.
- Evidence examples: reviewed spoken answer, written message, corrected sentence, or listening summary.
- Warning signs: meaning changes, repeated grammar errors, unclear pronunciation, or wrong vocabulary.

### Independence

- Meaning in English: learner can speak or write without excessive prompting, translation, or scripted dependence.
- Evidence examples: free speaking response, independent written artifact, or answer after minimal prompt.
- Warning signs: cannot continue without heavy hints or only repeats generated text.

### Explanation Quality

- Meaning in English: communication is clear, structured, relevant, and detailed enough for the audience.
- Evidence examples: technical explanation, interview answer, blocker explanation, or meeting contribution.
- Warning signs: unclear structure, missing context, too little detail, or memorized phrasing without meaning.

### Transfer Ability

- Meaning in English: learner can use language in new workplace, technical, interview, or writing contexts.
- Evidence examples: adapting an answer to a new question, explaining a new concept, or revising tone for audience.
- Warning signs: performance works only for rehearsed scripts.

### Consistency

- Meaning in English: learner performs reliably across repeated tasks and reduces recurring mistakes.
- Evidence examples: multiple reviewed outputs showing stable grammar, clarity, pronunciation, or vocabulary use.
- Warning signs: repeated same tense error, unclear pronunciation pattern, or recurring direct translation.

### Speed

- Meaning in English: response time and fluency matter in conversation and interviews when domain-appropriate.
- Evidence examples: timely answer with acceptable accuracy, smooth follow-up response, or reduced hesitation.
- Warning signs: fast but inaccurate speech, fluent memorized answers, or speed used to ignore clarity.

## Localization Notes

- Assessment feedback MAY be in Azerbaijani when learner or project preferences require it.
- English learner output SHOULD remain in English where appropriate.
- Localization preferences MUST NOT change evidence, mastery, or learner state semantics.
