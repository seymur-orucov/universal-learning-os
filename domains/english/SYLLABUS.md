# English Syllabus Skeleton

## Syllabus Overview

This is a draft syllabus skeleton for the `english` domain pack. It defines high-level progression only and does not create full lessons, dialogue banks, shadowing scripts, listening transcripts, exercise banks, or learner state.

Lesson completion does not imply mastery. Speaking exposure does not imply speaking mastery. Mastery requires evidence as defined in `core/mastery-model/EVIDENCE_REQUIREMENTS.md`.

## Syllabus Conventions

- Stages describe broad progression from B1 stabilization toward B2/C1 professional communication.
- Future lessons SHOULD reference learning skills from `domains/english/SKILL_GRAPH.md`.
- Practice SHOULD include observable learner output when progress or mastery may be updated.
- Learner progress belongs in learner state, not this syllabus.

## Draft Stages

### Stage 1: B1 Stabilization and Speaking Confidence

- Goal: Build confidence producing short, clear spoken answers about familiar work and life topics.
- Focus: self-introduction, daily routine speaking, simple explanations, and confidence under prompts.

### Stage 2: Core Grammar for Accurate Communication

- Goal: Stabilize high-frequency grammar used in professional speaking and writing.
- Focus: present forms, past forms, present perfect, future forms, questions, and modal verbs.

### Stage 3: Daily Work and Status Updates

- Goal: Communicate current work, progress, blockers, next steps, and requests clearly.
- Focus: standup-style updates, blocker explanations, clarifying questions, and concise follow-up.

### Stage 4: Vocabulary, Collocations, and Natural Phrasing

- Goal: Expand workplace and technical vocabulary while improving natural phrasing.
- Focus: collocations, phrase patterns, paraphrasing, and professional tone.

### Stage 5: Listening, Shadowing, and Pronunciation

- Goal: Improve comprehension, pronunciation clarity, rhythm, and confidence through listening and shadowing.
- Focus: main idea, details, pronunciation clarity, stress, intonation, and repeated output.

### Stage 6: Technical Explanation for Software Engineers

- Goal: Explain software engineering concepts, tradeoffs, decisions, and incidents clearly.
- Focus: structure, examples, cause/effect language, comparison, and technical vocabulary.

### Stage 7: Meetings, Discussions, and Feedback

- Goal: Participate in workplace discussions, ask questions, respond to feedback, and express disagreement professionally.
- Focus: meeting participation, clarification, turn-taking, feedback, and decision discussion.

### Stage 8: Writing for Work

- Goal: Write concise professional messages for remote and asynchronous work.
- Focus: emails, chat messages, PR comments, summaries, documentation notes, and tone control.

### Stage 9: Interview English

- Goal: Prepare for software engineering interviews in English.
- Focus: behavioral answers, technical explanations, follow-up questions, clarification, and structured responses.

### Stage 10: B2/C1 Fluency, Argumentation, and Nuance

- Goal: Improve nuanced communication, argumentation, and flexible expression.
- Focus: tradeoffs, uncertainty, prioritization, disagreement, and complex explanations.

### Stage 11: Speaking Portfolio and Real-World Practice

- Goal: Build reviewed artifacts from speaking, interview, workplace, and technical explanation practice.
- Focus: portfolio milestones, reviewed recordings or summaries, feedback cycles, and next actions.

### Stage 12: Review, Assessment, and Maintenance

- Goal: Maintain progress, target weak learning skills, and assess evidence-backed competence.
- Focus: review queue, repeated assessment, consistency checks, and maintenance practice.
