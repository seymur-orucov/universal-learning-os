# English Practice Rules

## Practice Overview

These draft rules define how the `english` domain pack maps framework practice types to English communication practice. They do not define full lessons, dialogue banks, shadowing scripts, listening transcripts, exercise banks, learner state, schemas, or executable tooling.

Practice rules SHOULD follow `core/learning-engine/PRACTICE_MODEL.md`.

## Practice Principles

- Practice SHOULD target learning skills from `domains/english/SKILL_GRAPH.md`.
- Speaking participation alone does not imply mastery.
- Learner output MUST be captured or summarized to become evidence.
- Practice completion alone MUST NOT create learner mastery records.
- Learner progress belongs in learner state, not this domain pack.

## Practice Type Mapping

### Recall Practice

- Purpose: Strengthen retrieval of grammar rules, vocabulary, collocations, phrase patterns, and interview structures.
- Suitable outputs: short answers, definitions, example sentences, or phrase substitutions.
- Evidence value: low to moderate; useful for recognition and understanding.

### Guided Practice

- Purpose: Build controlled production through sentence building, prompted answers, substitution drills, and scaffolded speaking.
- Suitable outputs: guided spoken answers, controlled written responses, or corrected sentence patterns.
- Evidence value: useful for M3 when feedback identifies what the learner can do with support.

### Independent Practice

- Purpose: Test free speaking, professional writing, technical explanations, or interview answers without heavy prompting.
- Suitable outputs: spoken response, written message, interview answer, or technical explanation.
- Evidence value: useful for M4 when reviewed for correctness, clarity, and independence.

### Mixed Practice

- Purpose: Combine grammar, vocabulary, listening, speaking, writing, and workplace context.
- Suitable outputs: role-play response, meeting summary, blocker explanation, or combined speaking/writing task.
- Evidence value: useful for transfer when multiple learning skills are demonstrated together.

### Error-Based Practice

- Purpose: Repair grammar, pronunciation, phrasing, vocabulary, clarity, or tone mistakes observed in learner output.
- Suitable outputs: corrected sentence, repeated spoken answer, revised message, or self-correction note.
- Evidence value: useful when the correction shows understanding and improved production.

### Project-Based Practice

- Purpose: Build speaking portfolio, interview portfolio, workplace communication portfolio, or writing portfolio artifacts.
- Suitable outputs: reviewed recording summary, written artifact, interview answer set, or technical explanation portfolio item.
- Evidence value: may support M5 when reviewed and connected to realistic communication contexts.

### Interview-Style Practice

- Purpose: Practice follow-up questions, technical explanations, behavioral answers, clarification, and pressure handling.
- Suitable outputs: answer, follow-up response, clarification question, or self-corrected response.
- Evidence value: may support M5 or M6 when responses are captured or summarized and evaluated.

## Localization Notes

- Instruction MAY be Azerbaijani when learner or project preferences require it.
- English examples, dialogues, phrases, vocabulary, and learner outputs SHOULD remain in English where appropriate.
- Localization behavior SHOULD follow `specification/LOCALIZATION_SPEC.md`.
